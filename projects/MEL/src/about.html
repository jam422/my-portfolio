83-Year-Old BettyResident for a year and a half.

She became interested in the residence after learning that it was an all-senior community, finding it very clean and friendly Since moving in the self-proclaimed retired home maker is feeling safer than ever in her cloister of fellow seniors. Betty says she is impressed with how well the grounds and common areas are kept, but it seems she is happiest with her new rhythm of life at Mel Hasty. In her neighborhood, Betty plays in several card clubs and often can be found at the Fond Du Lac Administration Building, attending many of their senior events. Within the building, she's kept busy with Bingo, potlucks, and musical events. Even though she has plenty to do at Mel Hasty, she likes the convenience of having all she needs in the neighborhood: grocery and drug stores, restau rants, gas stations, the local library, the fitness center across the street, and access to a major highway when the adventurous spirit strikes.

73-Year-Old BillResident for 10 years

This former restaurant manager likes to keep busy working out and partaking of the various activities around the residence, but he was initially drawn in by the build ing's convenient proximity to many community locations Bill is pretty keen on the computer room, where he can surf the internet at his convenience, and he says the com mon room is not only well appointed, but well kept. For him, the laundry room is a big plus, but the most pleasing thing he finds about the location isn't the features, it's the changes he's experienced since taking up residence, Bull describes himself as a loner before he took up residence, but it didn't take him long to take up with a tev residents he is now proud to call his friends,

86-Year-Old JuliaResident for 6 years.

The former bookkeeper, restaurant manager, and home nurse seems to have done it all and even found time to raise five children. She shows no sign of slowing down either. On top of painting, sewing drawing, cooking, and Bible study, Julia manages to tear through 6 to 12 books a week that's in addition to hosting an arts and crafts workshop in her apartment each week! What initially sparked Julia's interest in moving to Mel Hasty was its convenient location within the community and the look of the home and grounds she thought it looked so well kept and maintained that she returned for a second stay!
